## Quotes

> In mathematics, functions have no side effects. Consider the classic function 
> 
> $sin(x): y = sin(x)$
> 
 > No matter how much work $sin(x)$ does, all the results are returned and assigned to y. No global state of any kind is modified internally by $sin(x)$. Hence, we say that such a function is free of side effects, or **pure**.
 
 > The term lambda for anonymous functions originated in lambda calculus, where the greek letter lambda ($λ$) is used to represent anonymous functions.
